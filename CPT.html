College of Physical Therapy

OLFU's College of Physical Therapy remains as one of the most distinguished Physical Therapy schools in the country, producing numerous topnotchers in the Physical Therapist Licensure Examinations of the Professional Regulatory Commission (PRC), as well as in the Respiratory Therapist Licensure Examinations. It provides first-rate education through its faculty members mainly composed of licensed physicians who have specialized in their respective fields of expertise, registered physical therapists and respiratory therapists with master's degrees or units that have immersed them in teaching excellence and ethical patient care.

Its modern facilities, equipment, and updated curriculum provide top-quality training and instill unequalled proficiency and performance. Furthermore, it provides training programs that expose students to a variety of clinical settings through its partnerships and affiliations with government hospitals, private hospitals, and community-based centers.

Bachelor of Science in Physical Therapy

This is a four-year degree program that aims to develop competencies in different practice settings through high quality, value-based patient care outcomes. Specifically, the curriculum develops graduates who are competent, compassionate, ethical, professional, and service oriented. It supports the OLFU academic mission of "improving man as man" by educating skilled graduate level professionals in health care and rehabilitation services. It also equips future graduates in conducting research, engaging in lifelong learning and empowering community based partnerships.

Bachelor of Science in Respiratory Therapy

The Bachelor of Science in Respiratory Therapy program aims to produce graduates who are equipped with critical and analytical thinking skills and an enhanced knowledge in pulmonary care. The program provides students with an intensive foundation which will enable them to effectively assess, evaluate, treat, care, and educate patients with cardiopulmonary and breathing disorders.

The practice includes

  • Administration of drugs by inhalation Mechanical ventilator calibration, hooking, and management
  • Maintenance of airways
  • Assistance with cardiopulmonary resuscitation
  • Performing diagnostic procedures Function test and blood gas extraction and analysis
